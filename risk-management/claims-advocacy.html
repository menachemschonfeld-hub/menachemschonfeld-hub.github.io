Most brokers vanish when the claim is filed. That's not how we operate.

What it means

Claims advocacy is the discipline of representing the policyholder during the claims process. It is the work between filing the claim and resolving it — and it is the work most brokers don't do.

When you file a claim with SafeSide, your producer is on the file. We open the claim with the right department. We escalate when adjusters miss deadlines. We push back on coverage denials we believe are wrong. We coordinate with the adjuster, your attorney (if needed), and any third parties involved.

How we handle a typical claim

1. Initial intake — we capture the loss details, document everything in writing, and open the claim file with the carrier within 24 hours (often the same day).

2. Adjuster engagement — we make sure the right adjuster is assigned for your loss type. We've seen the same claim handled three different ways depending on adjuster experience.

3. Documentation support — we help you document the loss thoroughly. Insufficient documentation is the #1 cause of underpaid claims.

4. Coverage advocacy — if the carrier issues a reservation of rights or partial denial, we review the policy ourselves and challenge interpretations we disagree with.

5. Resolution — we stay on the file until the claim is closed, the check is cut, or we tell you why we can't get you further.

The hard cases

Most claims resolve cleanly. Some don't. When a carrier denies coverage, lowballs the loss, or drags the file beyond reasonable timelines, we escalate.

Sometimes that means going up the carrier's chain. Sometimes it means recommending public adjuster involvement. Sometimes it means recommending coverage counsel. We've never had a client face a hard claim alone.
