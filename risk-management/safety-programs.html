Workers' compensation mod management

Your experience modification factor — your mod — is the multiplier applied to your workers' comp premium based on your three-year loss history. A mod of 1.0 is average. 1.20 means you're paying 20% more than average. 0.85 means you're paying 15% less.

Most clients don't actively manage their mod. We do. We audit reserve estimates on open claims (often inflated, which inflates your mod), we ensure proper class code assignments, we coordinate return-to-work programs that close claims faster, and we benchmark your mod against industry peers.

OSHA preparation and compliance

OSHA inspections happen, and they tend to happen at the worst time. We help clients in OSHA-exposed industries (construction, manufacturing, healthcare) build documentation that survives an inspection — written safety programs, training records, OSHA 300 logs, hazard communication, and required postings.

Pre-inspection readiness is far cheaper than post-citation remediation.

Return-to-work programs

An injured employee on full disability is a much more expensive claim than an injured employee on light duty. Return-to-work programs identify modified-duty assignments before injuries happen and put injured employees back to work as soon as medically appropriate.

We help design these programs, document them, and coordinate with adjusters and treating physicians to keep claims moving.

Fleet safety and driver management

For clients with vehicle exposure, driver MVRs, vehicle telematics, and post-accident protocols are the most effective levers on commercial auto premium.

We coordinate with fleet management vendors, recommend telematics programs that produce premium credits, and review MVR pull schedules and disqualification protocols.
