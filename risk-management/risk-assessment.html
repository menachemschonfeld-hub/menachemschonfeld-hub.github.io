— Risk Assessment

Understand your exposures.

Comprehensive risk assessment — identifying exposures, gaps, and the right program structure before quoting begins.

Most brokers quote what you ask for. We start by understanding what you actually need to cover.

What's involved

A SafeSide risk assessment begins with a structured conversation — not a coverage application. Our producer walks through your operation, your assets, your contractual obligations, your historical losses, and the situations that worry you most.

From that conversation, we build a comprehensive exposure inventory. Every potential loss source is mapped. Every contractual liability is identified. Every regulatory requirement is logged.

What you get

A written exposure summary — what we've identified, organized by category and severity.

A coverage gap analysis — places where your current program is silent, sub-limited, or excluded.

A recommended program structure — limits, deductibles, layers, and specialty lines we believe are appropriate.

Carrier strategy — which markets are best positioned to write what you need, and where competitive tension exists.

Why it matters

Quoting without assessment is how programs get built around the wrong exposures. We've audited programs at renewal where the named insured was wrong, the limits hadn't been updated in seven years, the building values were 40% understated, and the cyber sub-limit was effectively meaningless.

An hour of structured assessment up front saves five hours of remediation at the first claim.

When to do this

Before binding a new program with SafeSide. Always.

When your operation has materially changed — acquisition, new location, new product line, new revenue scale.

After a near-miss event that exposed something your program didn't cover.

Every three-to-five years for stable operations, as a discipline.
