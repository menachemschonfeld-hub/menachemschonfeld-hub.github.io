— Overview

When the contract or license demands a bond.

Surety bonds aren't insurance — they're a financial guarantee. The surety company guarantees performance to a third party (the obligee) and you (the principal) ultimately pay back any loss. We work with multiple surety markets to place performance bonds, payment bonds, license bonds, and court bonds for contractors and licensed professionals.

— Frequently Asked

Common questions.

Insurance protects you. A bond protects the obligee (the third party requiring the bond). If a claim is paid, you ultimately reimburse the surety. Underwriting is more like a credit decision than insurance underwriting.

Contract bonds typically run 1–3% of the bond amount annually for well-qualified contractors. License and permit bonds are often a flat fee. Cost depends on credit, financials, and bond type.

Standard programs require no collateral for qualified applicants. Larger or harder-to-place bonds may require collateral or indemnity from owners.

License and permit bonds: same-day routine. Contract bonds: 1–5 business days, faster for established contractors with set bonding programs.
