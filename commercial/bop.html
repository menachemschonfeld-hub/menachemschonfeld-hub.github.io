— Overview

A BOP done right is a great place to start.

A Business Owners Policy bundles three core coverages — commercial property, general liability, and business interruption — into one streamlined policy. It's designed for small to mid-sized businesses with property at one or more locations. The BOP done right is foundational. The BOP done wrong leaves four gaps we see at every renewal.

— Frequently Asked

Common questions.

BOPs work for businesses under roughly 100 employees and modest revenues with manageable liability profiles. Larger or higher-risk operations typically need separate property and CGL policies.

Workers' comp (separate, often required by state). Auto (commercial auto needed). Professional services (E&O needed). Cyber breaches (cyber liability needed). Flood and earthquake (separate).

Calculate your gross profit (revenue minus variable costs) for 6–12 months. That's your minimum BI limit. Most businesses underinsure BI by 40%+.

Most BOPs offer a small cyber endorsement — but it's almost always inadequate ($25K–$100K). For real cyber protection, we recommend a standalone cyber policy.
