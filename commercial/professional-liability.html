— Overview

When the harm is financial, not physical.

General Liability covers physical harm — bodily injury, property damage. Professional Liability (also called Errors & Omissions or E&O) covers financial harm caused by your professional work — bad advice, missed deadlines, mistakes in deliverables. Critical for attorneys, accountants, consultants, designers, financial advisors, IT firms, and any professional whose work product carries client expectations.

GL covers physical harm — bodily injury, property damage. E&O covers financial harm — bad advice, missed deadlines, professional errors. Service businesses need both.

E&O is typically claims-made — coverage applies based on when the claim is made, not when the work was done. This makes the retroactive date and tail coverage critical, especially when changing carriers or retiring.

Extended Reporting Period — coverage for claims that surface after your policy ends. Essential when a professional retires, sells the practice, or changes carriers. We always discuss tail at every retirement or transition.
