About coverage

For most homeowners with a typical asset base, $1M to $2M of umbrella is appropriate. For higher-net-worth families, $5M to $25M+ is common. The rule of thumb: enough to cover your net worth plus future earnings.

Replacement cost, almost always. Actual cash value pays depreciated value, which is rarely enough to actually replace what you lost. The premium difference is small.

About 25% of NFIP flood claims come from properties outside high-risk flood zones. If you're near water, in a low-lying area, or in a place where heavy rain pools, consider it. Premium for low-risk zones is often $400 to $700 a year.

Occurrence policies cover incidents that happened during the policy period, regardless of when the claim is reported. Claims-made policies cover claims first reported during the policy period, regardless of when the incident happened. Most general liability is occurrence; most professional liability is claims-made.

The amount you pay out-of-pocket before insurance applies. Higher deductibles = lower premium. Most clients balance deductible and premium based on what they could comfortably absorb on a small claim.

About claims

Always report any incident that could become a claim, even if you don't end up pursuing payment. Late reporting is one of the most common reasons claims get denied. We'll help you decide whether to actually pursue payment based on the deductible and the impact on future premium.

It depends on the claim type, claim size, and carrier. Some carriers surcharge after the first claim; some don't until the second. Some claim types (catastrophic weather) are surcharge-free. We help clients evaluate the long-term cost of filing.
